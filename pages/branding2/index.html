Base Neutrals — Foundations & Backgrounds

Use for large areas, backgrounds, and to give your design breathing room.

Carbon Black – #1b1b1b Dark neutral for depth, softer than black.

Khaki Beige – #c3b091 Warm neutral for modern contrast.

Antique White – #faebd7 Warmer more inviting big cozy areas.

Parchment – #f4f0ec Light, warm neutral for softer backgrounds.

Primary Brand Colors — Core Identity

The main warm tones that define your look.

Rich Amber – #B45102 Deep, rich orange-brown for brand anchors.

Bright Ember – #E6783B Lighter, more vibrant orange for attention-grabbing accents.

Clay Terracotta – #C55A2B Midpoint tone for gradients or softer warm emphasis.

Secondary Accents — Contrast & Balance

Use sparingly to create visual interest and prevent monotony.

Slate Blue – #4A5D68 Cool, muted blue for contrast with warm tones.

Olive Bronze – #6B5B3D Earthy green-brown for grounded elements.

Deep Wine – #6A2E1B Dark, warm red-brown for depth and luxury notes.

Highlight & Prestige — Special Use Only

Small doses for high-value elements like tournament signage, premium products, or seasonal campaigns.

Warm Gold – #D9A441 Metallic-inspired highlight for prestige and elegance.

High-Impact Contrast — Clarity & Emphasis Only

Minimal use, mainly for text, outlines, or important highlights.

White – #FFFFFF For maximum contrast on dark backgrounds.

Black – #000000 For maximum contrast on light backgrounds.
